The body is the first laboratory. Before any system, before any notation, before any digital garden — there is the nervous system, doing its own composing. Every perception costs energy. Every interaction is a transaction in biological currency.

I've been tracking: the crashes follow the peaks with mechanical regularity. Not because I'm broken but because the instrument is sensitive. A Stradivarius doesn't survive a mosh pit. But it plays sounds no factory violin can dream of.

The question isn't whether to dampen the sensitivity. The question is: can I build scaffolding around it? Not to contain it but to catch what it produces before it dissipates.

That's what the system is. Not life management. Energy management. Catching what the mitochondria produce before the crash erases the memory of having produced it.
